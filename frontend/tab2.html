ABOUT THE PROJECT

Protecting endangered forests from illegal tree cutting or planting trees in areas that have already been deforested

The Kasigau Corridor in Kenya Project is a pioneer wildlife conservation project developed by Wildlife Works Carbon LLC. The project aims to protect a wildlife corridor of over 200,000 hectares of dryland forest with over 11,000 wild elephants that live in the local ecosystem. The area had been at threat of deforestation by local communities as a result of population growth and agricultural activity expansion.

Project impact to date:

11,000 elephants live in the protected project area

12,000 community members positively impacted by the project's social activities
